# Little Bible — Vocabulary Guide
**Version 1.0 | Established 2026-06-12**

---

## Vocabulary Tiers

Little Bible uses a three-tier vocabulary system.

| Tier | Definition | Usage |
|------|------------|-------|
| Tier 1 | Core vocabulary — always safe for ages 4–5 | Use freely in `little_bible` |
| Tier 2 | Familiar to most children — may need brief context | Use with care; introduce if new to the child |
| Tier 3 | Adult or archaic — must be replaced | Never use in `little_bible`, `memory_phrase`, `prayer`, or `discussion_question` |

---

## Tier 2 — Use With Care

These words are generally understood by ages 6–7 but may need brief context for ages 4–5. Use them, but be ready to explain.

```
wisdom       obedience    praise       worship
commandment  peace        grace        mercy
blessing     sin          creation     spirit
courage      promise      warning      consequence
rescue       covenant     holy         righteous
glory        shame        guilty       forgiven
humble       proud        servant      shepherd
temple       offering     sacrifice    faithful
```

**How to introduce a Tier 2 word:**

If a Tier 2 word appears in `little_bible`, either:
1. Follow it immediately with a brief explanation: `"Wisdom — knowing what is right."`
2. Or replace it with Tier 1 words that carry the same meaning.

## Names of God

Always use these forms in `little_bible`:

| KJV Form | Little Bible Form |
|----------|------------------|
| LORD (all caps) | God / the Lord |
| Lord GOD | the Lord God |
| Jehovah | God (or "the Lord") |
| Almighty | God who can do everything |
| Most High | the highest God / God above all |

---

## Names — Introduction Rule

On first mention, introduce any named person with a brief role:
> "A man named Solomon — he was a great king."
> "David — Solomon's father."
> "Moses — the man God chose to lead His people."

After the first mention, the name alone is sufficient.

---

## Numbers and Quantities

Use ordinary numbers in `little_bible`. Do not use archaic forms:
- Not: "threescore and ten"
- Use: "seventy"

Symbolic numbers (7, 12, 40) may appear without explanation in `little_bible` but should be explained in `meaning` and `parent_guide`.
