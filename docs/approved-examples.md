# Little Bible Approved Examples v1

These examples are fully compliant with Translation Charter v1 and Style Guide v1.
Use them as the reference standard when translating new chapters.

## Gold Standard Verses

### Proverbs 1:8 — Parental authority

**KJV:**
> My son, hear the instruction of thy father, and forsake not the law of thy mother:

**Little Bible (approved):**
> Listen to what your father teaches you. Do not forget what your mother says.

**Word counts:**
- S1: Listen(1) to(2) what(3) your(4) father(5) teaches(6) you(7). = 7 ✓
- S2: Do(1) not(2) forget(3) what(4) your(5) mother(6) says(7). = 7 ✓

**Memory phrase:** `Listen to your parents.` (4 words ✓)

**Prayer:** `God, help me obey my parents.` (6 words ✓)

**Discussion question:** `Why does God want us to obey parents?` (8 words ✓)

---

### Proverbs 1:10 — Peer pressure

**KJV:**
> My son, if sinners entice thee, consent thou not.

**Little Bible (approved):**
> If someone wants you to do wrong, say no.

**Word counts:**
- S1: If(1) someone(2) wants(3) you(4) to(5) do(6) wrong(7) say(8) no(9). = 9 — BORDERLINE
- Better: "If someone says to do wrong, say no." = 9 words — still borderline
- Best: "If someone asks you to do wrong: say no." — split into two thoughts
  - "If someone asks you to do wrong:" (7 words) + "Say no." (2 words) ✓

**Revised:**
> If someone asks you to do wrong, say no. That is always right.

**Memory phrase:** `Say no to wrong.` (4 words ✓)

**Prayer:** `God, help me say no to wrong.` (7 words ✓)

**Discussion question:** `How do you say no to wrong?` (7 words ✓)

### Proverbs 1:15 — Separation from evil companions

**KJV:**
> My son, walk not thou in the way with them; refrain thy foot from their path:

**Little Bible (approved):**
> Do not go with them. Stay on the right path.

**Word counts:**
- S1: Do(1) not(2) go(3) with(4) them(5). = 5 ✓
- S2: Stay(1) on(2) the(3) right(4) path(5). = 5 ✓

**Memory phrase:** `Stay on God's path.` (4 words ✓)

**Prayer:** `God, help me walk on Your path.` (7 words ✓)

**Discussion question:** `What is the brave thing when friends go wrong?` (9 words ✓)

### Proverbs 1:33 — Promise to the wise

**KJV:**
> But whoso hearkeneth unto me shall dwell safely, and shall be quiet from fear of evil.

**Little Bible (approved):**
> Those who listen to wisdom will be safe. They will not be afraid.

**Word counts:**
- S1: Those(1) who(2) listen(3) to(4) wisdom(5) will(6) be(7) safe(8). = 8 ✓
- S2: They(1) will(2) not(3) be(4) afraid(5). = 5 ✓

**Memory phrase:** `Wisdom keeps us safe.` (4 words ✓)

**Prayer:** `God, I want to listen to Your wisdom.` (8 words ✓)

**Discussion question:** `What does it feel like to be safe?` (8 words ✓)

## The "4-Year-Old Read-Aloud" Test

Read this sentence aloud to yourself, imagining a 4-year-old listening:

> "These sayings will teach you to do what is right, to be fair, and to treat people the way they should be treated."

Does a 4-year-old understand every part of that? No — too long, too much at once.

Now read this:

> "These sayings teach us to do what is right. They teach us to be fair to everyone."

Yes — clear, simple, two ideas separated.

Apply this test to every verse before approving.
