# Little Bible — Mission Document
**Version 1.0 | Established 2026-06-12 | Author: Oluwaseyi Adelaju**

---

## Why Little Bible Exists

Most children in the world will form their foundational beliefs about God before age seven.

The years from birth to age seven are the most important window in a person's spiritual formation. Yet most Bible resources for this age group are either:

- **Too simplified** — stripped of doctrine, consequence, and the full character of God
- **Too narrative** — story-based summaries that skip large portions of Scripture
- **Too abstract** — designed for adults reading to children, not children engaging with Scripture

Little Bible exists to fill this gap.

Little Bible is a **child-accessible Scripture adaptation and discipleship platform** built directly from the public-domain King James Version. It is not a story Bible. It is not a paraphrase. It is a faithful translation of every verse into language a 4–7-year-old can understand — while preserving the full theological content of the source text.

---

## The Core Problem We Are Solving

**Problem 1: Most young children do not have access to the Bible in their own language.**
Even where translations exist, they are written for adults or older children. A 4-year-old cannot engage with the NIV or ESV. They need language written at their level — not just simplified, but *faithfully adapted*.

**Problem 2: Children are being discipled by the world before the church reaches them.**
The average child spends thousands of hours with media before age seven. Little Bible competes for that time with Scripture-rooted content.

**Problem 3: Discipleship is being outsourced to Sunday school — not owned by parents.**
The Bible gives the primary discipleship role to parents (Deuteronomy 6:4–9). Little Bible is a tool that puts daily Scripture in the hands of families.

## Mission Statement

> **To help every child understand, love, remember, and obey God's Word from their earliest years.**

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Children aged 4–5
- Being read to by a parent or caregiver
- Beginning to recognize simple words
- Can repeat short phrases from memory
- Understand concrete ideas, not abstractions
- Respond to warmth, repetition, and clear cause-and-effect

### Secondary: Children aged 6–7
- Emerging independent readers
- Can follow a sentence if vocabulary is simple
- Beginning to ask "why" questions about faith
- Ready for slightly more nuanced memory phrases

### Tertiary: Parents and caregivers
- Using Little Bible as a daily devotional tool with their child
- Not necessarily theologians — need guidance on discussion questions
- Need content that is both child-accessible and theologically trustworthy

### Quaternary: Content reviewers and translators
- Checking theological fidelity
- Translating into other languages
- Maintaining the standard across all books

---

## Desired Outcomes

Every child who grows up with Little Bible should:

1. **Know God** — Have a clear, age-appropriate understanding of who God is: His love, holiness, power, faithfulness, and justice.

2. **Love God** — Not just know facts about God, but have a heart drawn toward Him.

3. **Trust God** — Know that God keeps His promises. Know that His Word is true.

4. **Obey God** — Understand that obedience is not optional and that it flows from love.

5. **Pray to God** — Have a natural habit of talking to God as a real Person.

6. **Memorize Scripture** — Carry the Word in their hearts, not just their heads.

7. **Follow Jesus** — Understand that Jesus is the center of Scripture and the only way to God.

## Discipleship Philosophy

### 1. Scripture is sufficient
We do not add to Scripture. We make it accessible. The goal is always that the child is engaging with *God's Word*, not a creative retelling.

### 2. Doctrine belongs to children
Sin, judgment, grace, repentance, the fear of the Lord — these are not adult concepts. They are human concepts. Children experience all of them. We do not hide doctrine. We translate it.

### 3. Parents are the primary disciplers
Little Bible is a tool for families. Every chapter includes:
- A parent guide (theological depth for the adult)
- Discussion questions (conversation starters)
- Application for children (one practical response)

The platform serves the family, not the individual child in isolation.

### 4. Repetition forms character
Memory phrases are short precisely so a child can repeat them. Prayer is short precisely so a child can pray it daily. Repetition over years builds belief.

### 5. Coverage matters
The goal is all 66 books. Not just the "child-friendly" parts. Not just the familiar stories. Every verse. Because God said every word was profitable for doctrine, reproof, correction, and instruction in righteousness (2 Timothy 3:16).

- Audio read-aloud narration
- Scripture memory tools
- Family devotion guides
- Discipleship journeys (curriculum tracks)
- Multiple languages
- A global children's discipleship platform
