# Little Bible — Style Guide
**Version 1.0 | Established 2026-06-12**

---

## The Primary Test

Before approving any verse adaptation, ask:

> *"If I read this aloud to a 4-year-old right now, would they understand every single word?"*

Yes → may proceed.
No → simplify and re-test.

---

## Part 1 — Sentence Construction

### Approved Sentence Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| God + verb + object | "God loves you." |
| Subject + short verb | "Wise people listen." |
| Command (imperative) | "Say no to wrong." |
| Simple condition | "If you obey, you will be safe." |
| Simple contrast | "Bad choices hurt us. Good choices help us." |
| Observation | "Wisdom is like a person calling out." |
| Short cause-effect | "They did wrong. Trouble came." |

### Prohibited Sentence Constructions

| Construction | Why prohibited | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Two ideas joined with "and" over 8 words | Too long, too complex | Split into two sentences |
| Passive voice for theological truth | Removes agency and clarity | Use active voice |
| Abstract noun as subject | Too abstract for age 4 | Replace with concrete subject |
| "It can be said that..." | Academic, not conversational | State directly |
| "One might..." | Distant, uncertain | "We can..." or just state it |
| Parenthetical additions | Breaks reading flow | Move to separate sentence |

## Part 2 — Breaking Complex Verses

### The Three-Step Method

**Step 1:** Read the KJV verse and list every distinct idea.

**Step 2:** Write one sentence per idea.

**Step 3:** Check each sentence: ≤ 8 words? One idea? Passes comprehension test?

### Full Worked Examples

---

**Proverbs 1:2** (KJV):
> "To know wisdom and instruction; to perceive the words of understanding;"

*Distinct ideas:*
1. Learning wisdom
2. Understanding what is right

*Draft:*
> "These sayings help us learn wisdom. They show us what is right."

*Check:*
- S1: "These sayings help us learn wisdom." = 6 words ✓
- S2: "They show us what is right." = 7 words ✓
- Both pass comprehension test ✓

---

**Proverbs 1:3** (KJV):
> "To receive the instruction of wisdom, justice, and judgment, and equity;"

*Distinct ideas:*
1. Learning to do right
2. Being fair to people

*Draft:*
> "These sayings teach us to do what is right. They teach us to be fair to everyone."

*Check:*
- S1: "These sayings teach us to do what is right." = 9 words ✗ (too long)
- Revised S1: "These sayings teach us what is right." = 7 words ✓
- S2: "They teach us to be fair to everyone." = 8 words ✓

*Final:*
> "These sayings teach us what is right. They teach us to be fair to everyone."

---

**Proverbs 1:7** (KJV):
> "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge: but fools despise wisdom and instruction."

*Distinct ideas:*
1. Respecting God is where wisdom begins
2. Foolish people reject wisdom

*Draft:*
> "Respecting God is where wisdom begins. People who ignore God are foolish."

*Check:*
- S1: "Respecting God is where wisdom begins." = 6 words ✓
- S2: "People who ignore God are foolish." = 6 words ✓
- Both pass ✓

## Part 3 — Memory Phrase Style

### Rules
- **Maximum 4 words. Hard limit. No exceptions.**
- Complete thought — not a sentence fragment.
- Theologically accurate — not just inspiring.
- Positive phrasing preferred where the verse is positive.
- Command or statement — not a question.
- No punctuation required beyond a period.

### Construction Methods

| Method | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Short command | "Say no to wrong." |
| Short statement | "Wisdom keeps us safe." |
| Subject + verb | "Choices have results." |
| God + verb + me | "God helps me understand." |
| Imperative + object | "Listen to your parents." |
| Noun + verb phrase | "Wisdom starts with God." |

### Word Count Guide

The 4-word limit is strict. Count every word:

> "Say no to wrong." = Say(1) no(2) to(3) wrong(4) ✓
> "Wisdom keeps us safe." = Wisdom(1) keeps(2) us(3) safe(4) ✓
> "God, help me choose right." = God(1) help(2) me(3) choose(4) right(5) ✗ — 5 words

When a phrase is 5 words, find the 4-word version:
- "God helps me choose." ✓
- "Choose what is right." ✓
- "God, help me now." ✓

## Part 4 — Prayer Style

### Rules
- Begin with `God,` — always. Never `Dear God,`.
- **Maximum 10 words.**
- Must be a genuine prayer — request or thanksgiving.
- Must connect to this specific verse's message.
- Must sound natural when spoken by a 4-year-old.

### Word Count Reference

> "God, help me obey You." = God(1) help(2) me(3) obey(4) You(5) = 5 words ✓
> "God, help me trust You in hard times." = God(1) help(2) me(3) trust(4) You(5) in(6) hard(7) times(8) = 8 words ✓
> "God, thank You that Your wisdom is for me too." = God(1) thank(2) You(3) that(4) Your(5) wisdom(6) is(7) for(8) me(9) too(10) = 10 words ✓ (maximum)

### Prayer Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Help + verb | "God, help me choose right." |
| Help + be + adjective | "God, help me be kind." |
| Keep + path | "God, keep me on Your path." |
| Teach + what | "God, teach me Your wisdom." |
| Thank + for | "God, thank You for wise teachers." |
| I want + to | "God, I want to listen to You." |

## Part 5 — Discussion Question Style

### Rules
- **Maximum 10 words.**
- **One question only.** Never a compound question.
- Must be answerable by a child who heard only the `little_bible` text.
- Must open a natural parent-child conversation.
- No yes/no questions if they can be avoided.

### Preferred Question Starters
- "What..."
- "Who..."
- "Why..."
- "How..."
- "Can you..."

### Examples by Verse Topic

| Verse topic | Approved question |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Learning wisdom | "What does being wise mean?" |
| Obeying parents | "Why does God want us to obey parents?" |
| Peer pressure | "How do you say no to wrong?" |
| Fear of the Lord | "How do we show respect to God?" |
| Safety in God | "What does it feel like to be safe?" |
| Consequences | "Can you name a good or bad choice result?" |
| Repentance | "What does it feel like to do right?" |
| Greed | "Why is always wanting more a bad thing?" |

## Part 6 — Meaning Field Style

The `meaning` field has no word limit. Written at adult level.

**Structure:**
1. State the theological content of the verse in plain language.
2. Explain any significant nuance or difficulty.
3. Cross-reference to related passages if helpful.
4. Explain the practical implication for a child's life.

**Tone:** Pastoral, clear, not academic. A thoughtful parent should find it helpful without needing a seminary degree.

---

## Part 7 — Parent Guide Style

One per chapter. 2–4 paragraphs.

**Paragraph 1:** The main theological theme of the chapter.
**Paragraph 2:** The most important thing for a parent to communicate to their child.
**Paragraph 3:** Age-appropriate application or activity.
**Paragraph 4 (optional):** Cross-references or deeper study pointers.

**Tone:** Like a trusted pastor or Bible teacher speaking to a parent who loves their child and wants to do the right thing — not condescending, not overly technical.
