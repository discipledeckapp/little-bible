# Little Bible Style Guide v1

**Status: Official — applies to all books, all languages, all future chapters**

---

## The Single Test

Before approving any verse adaptation, ask:

> If I read this aloud to a 4-year-old right now, would they understand every word?

If yes — approved.
If no — simplify.

---

## Sentence Construction

### The Pattern

Most Little Bible sentences follow one of these patterns:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| God + verb + object | God loves you. |
| Subject + short verb | Wise people listen. |
| Command | Say no to wrong. |
| Simple condition | If you obey, you are safe. |
| Simple contrast | Bad choices hurt us. Good choices help us. |

### Length Rules

- **Maximum:** 8 words per sentence.
- **Target:** 3–6 words.
- **Never chain** two ideas with "and" into one sentence if it exceeds 8 words. Break it into two sentences.

**Wrong:**
> These sayings will help you learn wisdom and understand what is right.
(12 words — too long)

**Right:**
> These sayings help us learn wisdom. They show us what is right.
(both sentences ≤ 8 words ✓)

---

## Breaking Complex Verses

When a KJV verse contains multiple ideas:

1. Identify each distinct idea.
2. Assign one sentence to each idea.
3. Each sentence must pass the 8-word rule.
4. Read them all together — they must flow naturally aloud.

**KJV Example (Proverbs 1:2):**
> "To know wisdom and instruction; to perceive the words of understanding;"

**Incorrect Little Bible:**
> These sayings will help you learn wisdom and understand what is right.

**Correct Little Bible:**
> These sayings help us learn wisdom. They show us what is right.

---

## Voice and Tone

**Always:**
- Warm, direct, gentle.
- Present tense where possible.
- Active voice.
- Second person ("you") or first person plural ("we") for relatable connection.

**Never:**
- Cold or academic.
- Passive voice for theological statements.
- Third person distance when direct connection is possible.

**Wrong:** "It is said that the wise will be kept safe."
**Right:** "Wise people are kept safe by God."

---

## Memory Phrase Construction

The memory phrase is the most important field for a child.

Rules:
- **Exactly 1–4 words.** No exceptions.
- Must be a complete, self-contained truth.
- Must be memorable when spoken aloud.
- Must directly reflect the verse's core truth — not a summary of all ideas.
- Write it as something a child can say to themselves.

**Testing a memory phrase:**
Ask: "If a child repeats only this phrase tomorrow, will they remember a true thing about God or life?"

| Verse topic | Memory phrase |
|-------------|---------------|
| Wisdom begins with God | Wisdom starts with God. |
| Obey parents | Listen to your parents. |
| Say no to peer pressure | Say no to wrong. |
| Greed is destructive | Greed always hurts you. |
| God calls to us | God is calling. Listen. |
| Wisdom brings safety | Wisdom keeps us safe. |
| Choices have consequences | Choices have results. |
| Fear of the Lord | God first. That is wisdom. |

## Prayer Construction

- Begin with: `God,`
- Never begin with "Dear God" — this adds 1 word unnecessarily and sounds older/formal.
- Follow with a simple request directly tied to the verse.
- End with a period.
- **Maximum 10 words total.**

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Help + verb | God, help me choose right. |
| Help + be + adjective | God, help me be kind. |
| Keep + pronoun + path | God, keep me on Your path. |
| Teach + what | God, teach me Your wisdom. |
| Thank + for | God, thank You for wise teachers. |

**Counting words in prayers:**
> God(1), help(2) me(3) choose(4) right(5). = 5 words ✓
> God(1), help(2) me(3) be(4) kind(5) to(6) everyone(7) I(8) meet(9). = 9 words ✓
> God(1), thank(2) You(3) for(4) the(5) wisdom(6) in(7) Your(8) Word(9). = 9 words ✓

---

## Discussion Question Construction

- One question only. Never two.
- **Maximum 10 words.**
- Must be answerable by a child who has not read the adult meaning — based on the child-level verse alone.
- Prefer open questions, not yes/no.
- Start with: Who, What, Why, How, When, Can you.

**Testing a discussion question:**
Read only the `little_bible` field to a child and then ask the question. Can they attempt an answer? If yes — approved.

---

## Meaning Field

The `meaning` field is for parents and reviewers — not shown in Child Mode.

- No word limit.
- Should explain the theological depth for an adult.
- May reference the KJV and cross-references.
- Must be accurate and unabridged theologically.
- Written at adult level.

---

## Parent Guide

Written for parents/teachers — one per chapter, not per verse.

- Practical, warm, pastoral tone.
- Include: age-appropriate activity, prayer focus, cross-reference points.
- Maximum 4 paragraphs.
- No academic or seminary language.

---

## Application for Children

One per chapter. Written at parent level (parent reads it and leads the child through it).

- One practical activity for the week.
- Must be doable by a 4-year-old with adult support.
- Maximum 3 sentences.

---

## Handling Difficult Passages

Some passages contain violence, sexual content, or theological complexity.

### Violence
- Preserve the warning. Remove graphic detail.
- Always explain consequence in child-level terms.
- Never make violence exciting or detailed.

### Adult themes
- Focus on the moral/theological principle, not the specific act.
- Example: adultery → "doing something wrong that breaks a family apart" or hold for age 8+ curriculum.

### Wrath of God
- Never soften to remove God's justice.
- Frame as: "God is fair. Wrong choices have results."
- Never make God seem cruel — explain His justice as love.

### Death
- "They got hurt very badly." / "It ended very badly for them."
- For passages like Psalms that reference death as comfort: "God is with us even in sad times."

---

## Checklist Before Finalizing Any Verse

- [ ] Every `little_bible` sentence ≤ 8 words
- [ ] Preferred sentence length 3–6 words
- [ ] One idea per sentence
- [ ] No prohibited words (see vocabulary guide)
- [ ] `memory_phrase` ≤ 4 words
- [ ] `prayer` ≤ 10 words, starts with "God,"
- [ ] `discussion_question` ≤ 10 words, answerable by a 4-year-old
- [ ] Core theological content of KJV verse preserved
- [ ] Sin, consequence, command, promise — none removed
- [ ] Child comprehension test passed
