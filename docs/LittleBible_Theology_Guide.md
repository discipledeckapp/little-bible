# Little Bible — Theology Guide
**Version 1.0 | Established 2026-06-12**

---

> This guide defines how every major theological concept must be handled across all 66 books.
> It ensures that a child reading Proverbs receives the same theological foundation as a child reading John.
> Consistency is not optional. It is how children form a coherent faith.

---

## 1. God

### What children must know
- God is real. He is not a character in a story.
- God made everything.
- God loves us deeply and completely.
- God is holy — He is perfect and cannot be okay with wrong.
- God is all-powerful. Nothing is too hard for Him.
- God knows everything. He always sees us.
- God is always present. He is with us everywhere.
- God is faithful. He always keeps His promises.
- God is good — always. Even in hard things.
- God is just — He always does what is right.

### Approved child-level language
```
God loves you.
God made everything.
God always keeps His promises.
God sees everything.
God is always with us.
God is good.
God is holy — that means He is perfectly good.
Nothing is too hard for God.
```

### Prohibited framings
- ❌ "God might..." or "God sometimes..." (He is not uncertain)
- ❌ "God was upset" without explanation of why (always explain His justice)
- ❌ Presenting God as a distant or uninvolved figure
- ❌ Omitting God's holiness when translating passages that emphasize it

## 2. Jesus Christ

### What children must know
- Jesus is God's Son.
- Jesus came to earth as a real person.
- Jesus died for our sins — He took the punishment we deserved.
- Jesus came back to life. Death could not hold Him.
- Jesus is alive today.
- Jesus loves children specifically (Mark 10:14).
- Jesus is the only way to God (John 14:6).
- Jesus will come back one day.

### In Old Testament passages
The `little_bible` field does not artificially insert Jesus into OT passages.
The `meaning` and `parent_guide` fields may draw connections to Christ where the passage is genuinely messianic or foreshadowing.

### New Testament passages
Jesus must be presented with full divinity and full humanity. Never present Him as merely a good teacher or example. He is Lord.

### Approved child-level language
```
Jesus is God's Son.
Jesus loves us.
Jesus died so our sins could be forgiven.
Jesus came back to life.
Jesus is alive.
Jesus is the way to God.
Jesus loves children.
```

## 3. Salvation

### What children must know
- All people do wrong. This separates us from God.
- God loves us and does not want us to stay separated from Him.
- Jesus died to pay for our sins.
- We can be forgiven and come back to God through Jesus.
- Salvation is a gift — we cannot earn it.
- When we trust Jesus, we are part of God's family.

### Critical rule
**Never present salvation as being earned through good behavior.** Even in wisdom literature (Proverbs, Psalms), the call to obedience is a response to God's grace — not the means of earning it. The `meaning` and `parent_guide` must make this clear when relevant.

### Approved child-level language
```
Jesus died so we could be forgiven.
God can make us right with Him because of Jesus.
When we trust Jesus, God forgives all our wrong.
Being part of God's family is a gift.
We cannot be good enough on our own. We need Jesus.
```

---

## 4. Sin

### What children must know
- Sin is choosing to do wrong against God.
- Everyone sins. No one is perfect except God.
- Sin has consequences — it hurts us and those around us.
- Sin separates us from God.
- God hates sin but loves us.
- Sin can be forgiven through Jesus.

### Critical rules
- **Never remove sin from any verse where it appears.**
- **Never reframe sin as merely a mistake or accident.** Sin involves a choice.
- **Never present sin without the possibility of forgiveness.** (In passages where forgiveness is not the focus, the `meaning` field may note it.)
- Do not make children feel guilty without also giving them hope.

### Approved child-level language
```
We all do wrong sometimes.
Doing wrong is called sin.
Sin makes God sad because He is holy.
Sin always has results.
When we sin, we can ask God to forgive us.
God hates wrong, but He loves us.
```

## 5. Obedience

### What children must know
- God asks us to obey Him.
- Obeying God is a way of showing love (John 14:15).
- Obedience is not just following rules — it is trusting that God's way is best.
- God also calls children to obey their parents (Ephesians 6:1).
- Obedience does not always feel easy, but it is always right.
- Disobedience has consequences.

### Critical rule
**Obedience must always be connected to love, not fear alone.** Children should obey God because they love Him and trust Him — not merely because they are afraid of punishment.

### Approved child-level language
```
When we love God, we want to obey Him.
Obeying God shows that we trust Him.
God's way is the best way.
Obey your parents — God says to.
Doing what God says is right, even when it's hard.
```

## 6. Wisdom

### What children must know
- Wisdom is knowing what is right and doing it.
- Wisdom starts with respecting God (Proverbs 1:7).
- Wisdom helps us make good choices.
- Wisdom is not the same as being smart — it is knowing and following God's ways.
- Wise people keep on learning.
- God gives wisdom to those who ask.

### The Fear of the Lord Rule
The "fear of the Lord" is the foundation of wisdom. It must never be removed or softened into mere friendliness. See the Translation Charter for the full policy.

### Approved child-level language
```
Wisdom is knowing what is right.
Wise people respect God.
God gives wisdom to those who ask.
Wisdom helps us choose well.
Learning God's ways is wisdom.
```

---

## 7. Judgment and Consequences

### What children must know
- God is just — He always does what is right.
- Our choices have consequences. This is not cruelty — it is fairness.
- Bad choices lead to bad results.
- God disciplines those He loves (Hebrews 12:6).
- Judgment is not the final word for those who trust God — mercy and grace are.

### Critical rule
**Never remove consequences from a verse.** Children must understand that choices matter. Softening consequences teaches that sin is harmless, which is a theological error.

### Approved child-level language
```
Bad choices have results.
God is fair. Wrong things always have a cost.
When we do wrong, we feel the consequences.
God corrects us because He loves us.
God will make all things right in the end.
```

## 8. Heaven

### What children must know
- Heaven is where God lives.
- Heaven is a real place.
- Those who trust Jesus will be with God forever.
- Heaven will have no more pain, sadness, or death.
- Being with God forever is the best thing there is.

### Approved child-level language
```
Heaven is where God lives.
Heaven is a beautiful place.
Those who love God will be with Him forever.
There is no sadness or pain in heaven.
Being with God is the best thing ever.
```

---

## 9. Hell

### Age-appropriate handling
For ages 4–7, hell is presented as:
- The place of separation from God.
- The consequence of rejecting God.
- The thing Jesus came to save us from.

Graphic depictions of torment are not appropriate for ages 4–5. The `little_bible` field should use:
- "A place far from God."
- "Separated from God forever."
- "The place no one wants to be."

The `meaning` and `parent_guide` fields may be more theologically precise for parents.

**Critical rule: Never remove hell from passages where it appears.** Adapt the language appropriately, but preserve the reality of the consequence.

## 10. Repentance

### What children must know
- Repentance means turning away from wrong and turning back to God.
- Repentance is a gift from God — He draws us to Himself.
- Repentance is not just feeling sorry — it includes changing direction.
- God welcomes those who repent with open arms (Luke 15:20).

### Approved child-level language
```
Turning back to God is called repentance.
When we do wrong, we can stop and say sorry to God.
God is always waiting for us to come back to Him.
Turn away from wrong. Turn back to God.
God forgives everyone who really turns to Him.
```

---

## 11. Faith

### What children must know
- Faith means trusting God even when we cannot see Him.
- Faith means believing that what God says is true.
- Faith is not just believing facts — it is trusting a Person.
- God is pleased when we trust Him (Hebrews 11:6).
- Faith grows as we read God's Word and see Him act.

### Approved child-level language
```
Faith means trusting God.
God is pleased when we trust Him.
We cannot see God, but He is real.
Believing what God says — that is faith.
Faith grows when we read God's Word.
```

---

## 12. Prayer

### What children must know
- Prayer is talking to God.
- God always hears our prayers.
- We can pray anywhere, any time.
- Prayer is not a formula — it is a conversation.
- God answers prayer — sometimes yes, sometimes no, sometimes wait.
- Prayer is an act of trust.

### Prayer in Little Bible content
Every verse has a `prayer` field — max 10 words, starting with `God,`. This is not a liturgical prayer. It is a child's natural response to the truth of the verse.

### Approved child-level language
```
Prayer is talking to God.
God always hears you when you pray.
You can talk to God anywhere.
God answers our prayers.
Praying is a way of trusting God.
```

## Cross-Book Consistency Rules

1. **God's character is consistent across all 66 books.** The God of Proverbs is the same God of Revelation. Never adapt content in a way that makes God seem different across books.

2. **Jesus is present throughout Scripture.** Old Testament passages should not mention Jesus in `little_bible`, but `meaning` and `parent_guide` may draw Christ-centered connections where appropriate (types and shadows, messianic prophecy, etc.).

3. **Sin is always serious. Grace is always available.** This tension must be maintained in every book, every chapter.

4. **Wisdom literature, law, poetry, prophecy, history, epistle, and apocalyptic literature all serve the same God.** Adapt the genre for children — but never lose the genre's distinctive voice or purpose.
