# Little Bible Vocabulary Guide v1

**Status: Official — applies to all books, all languages, all future chapters**

---

## Tier 1 — Always Use (age 4–5 core vocabulary)

These words are safe for any sentence without explanation.

### About God
God, Lord, Jesus, Holy Spirit, Father, heaven, angels, love, good, holy, great, true, helper, king

### Actions
hear, listen, obey, pray, help, give, share, tell, ask, learn, grow, come, go, stop, say, do, make, see, know, choose, keep, turn, find, hold, trust, follow, love, care, speak, call

### Character
wise, kind, good, right, safe, strong, brave, fair, gentle, true, faithful, thankful, joyful

### Opposites (sin side)
bad, wrong, foolish, mean, cruel, greedy, proud, angry, afraid, hurt, sad, lost

### Nouns
heart, life, path, way, word, words, voice, hand, home, family, father, mother, child, friend, people, land, king, house, day, night, time

### Connector words
and, but, so, when, if, then, because, not, too, also, even, still

---

## Tier 2 — Use With Care (may need one-word explanation)

These words are safe but may need context for the youngest readers.

wisdom, obedience, praise, worship, commandment, peace, grace, mercy, blessing, sin, wrong, evil, creation, spirit, courage, promise, warning, consequence

**How to introduce a Tier 2 word:**
> "Wisdom — that means knowing what is good and right."

happy, sad, scared, afraid, angry, glad, worried, safe, loved, brave, proud (negative: too proud), lonely, thankful, joyful, sorry

## Prohibited Phrases

These constructions are banned even if the individual words are allowed:

- "It can be understood that…"
- "One might say…"
- "In a metaphorical sense…"
- "This is symbolic of…"
- Any phrase requiring a child to understand metaphor without explanation
- Any passive voice construction over 5 words
- Any sentence beginning with "The fact that…"

---

## Names

Use names as written in KJV unless there is a widely accepted child-level alternative.

Always introduce a named person with a brief role identifier on first mention:
> "A man named Solomon — he was a king."
> "David — Solomon's father and a great king."

Thereafter the name alone is sufficient.
