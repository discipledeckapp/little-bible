# Little Bible Translation Charter v1

**Status: Official — applies to all books, all languages, all future chapters**

---

## Mission

Help very young children understand God's Word without losing the original meaning.

The Little Bible is not a children's story Bible. It is a child-accessible Scripture adaptation based on the public-domain KJV.

---

## Primary Audience

**Age 4–5** — being read to by a parent or older sibling.

**Secondary audience — Age 6–7** — emerging readers, may read independently.

Every verse must be understandable when read aloud to a 4-year-old.

---

## Core Principles (in priority order)

1. Meaning before wording.
2. Simplicity before elegance.
3. Faithfulness before creativity.
4. Child understanding before adult sophistication.
5. Preserve doctrine.
6. Preserve warnings.
7. Preserve promises.
8. Preserve commands.
9. Preserve God's character.
10. Preserve the Gospel.

---

## Reading Level

**Target: Age 4–5**

- Every verse must be understandable when read aloud.
- Most verses must be readable by an emerging reader.

### Sentence Rules

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Maximum words per sentence | 8 |
| Preferred sentence length | 3–6 words |
| Ideas per sentence | 1 |
| Paragraphs | Not permitted |
| Complex verses | Break into multiple short sentences |

---

## Field-Level Rules

### `little_bible`

- Max 8 words per sentence. Prefer 3–6.
- One idea per sentence.
- Multiple sentences allowed. Each must pass on its own.
- No long paragraphs.
- Must preserve the full theological content of the source verse.
- Run child comprehension test before finalizing (see below).

### `memory_phrase`

- **Maximum 4 words.**
- Must be a complete, memorable thought.
- Must directly reflect the verse's core truth.
- Designed to be memorized by a 4-year-old.

**Approved examples:**
- Listen to God.
- Choose what is right.
- Love God's wisdom.
- Stay on God's path.
- God helps me understand.
- Say no to wrong.

### `prayer`

- **Maximum 10 words.**
- Begin with: `God,`
- Use simple verbs: help, teach, show, keep, give, make.
- Must connect directly to the verse's message.

**Approved examples:**
- God, help me obey You.
- God, help me choose right.
- God, keep me on Your path.
- God, teach me Your wisdom.

### `discussion_question`

- **Maximum 10 words.**
- Must be answerable by a 4-year-old.
- Use simple question words: Who, What, Why, How, When.
- One question per verse. No multi-part questions.

**Approved examples:**
- Who helps you learn?
- What is the right choice?
- Why should we obey God?
- How can we show love today?

---

## Doctrinal Rules

### Fear of the Lord

Never remove this concept.

Approved translations:
- God is great. Honor Him. That is wisdom.
- Respect God. That is where wisdom begins.
- Love God. Listen to Him. That is wisdom.

Never soften to: "be a little scared of God" or omit entirely.

### Sin

Never remove sin from any verse.

Approved terms: doing wrong, disobeying God, choosing the wrong path, bad choices.

Never replace with: making mistakes, accidents, poor decisions (alone).

### Repentance

Approved terms: turn back, stop doing wrong, come back to God, turn around.

### Judgment and Consequences

Never remove consequences.

Approved child-level framing:
- When we do wrong, bad things happen.
- God warns us because He loves us.
- Choices always come back to us.

### Death / Destruction

When the source verse contains death or severe destruction:

- Preserve the warning without graphic detail.
- Use: "They got hurt very badly." / "It ended badly for them."
- Never remove the consequence.

---

## Child Comprehension Test

Before finalizing any verse, ask:

> Could a typical 4-year-old understand this when read aloud?

If not — simplify again.

---

## Consistency Rule

Before translating a new chapter:

1. Review the most recent approved chapter.
2. Match the vocabulary level.
3. Match the sentence style.
4. Match the theological standards.
5. Match the memory phrase format (max 4 words).

---

## Prohibited Words (without immediate explanation)

understanding, instruction, perceive, discretion, counsel, interpretation, covetousness, prosperity, reproof, subtilty, equity, concourse, uttereth, crieth, hearkeneth
